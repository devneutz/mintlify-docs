---
title: "Administering an Event"
description: "Manage signups, shifts, and sharing for a live event"
---

Once an event is [created and published](/en/guides/creating-an-event), most of
your work happens on that event's admin page: watching shifts fill up,
helping volunteers who couldn't sign up themselves, and making corrections.

## Monitoring signups

Each shift on the event page shows its volunteers and a `filled / capacity`
count (e.g. `3 / 4`). Volunteers who signed up themselves appear with their
name and email; anyone you added manually is marked **added manually**.

## Adding a volunteer manually

Useful when someone tells you in person or by phone instead of using the
sign-up page.

1. Open the shift and expand **+ Add volunteer**.
2. Enter their name, email, and (optionally) email language and team.
3. Submit — they receive the same confirmation email as a self-service
   signup, including their manage link.

## Moving a signup to another shift

If a volunteer needs to swap shifts, use **Move to…** next to their name,
pick the target shift, and confirm with **Move**. They're notified by email
of the change.

## Assigning a team

If your club uses teams, set or change a signup's team from the **Team**
dropdown next to their name, then **Save team**. This only affects
reporting (see [Statistics](#statistics)) — it doesn't require the
volunteer to take any action.

## Removing a signup

Click **Remove** next to a volunteer, confirm, and their slot opens back up.
They're notified by email that their signup was removed.

<Warning>
  Volunteers can also cancel their own signup or erase their data at any
  time via the manage link in their confirmation email — you don't need to
  do this for them unless they ask you to.
</Warning>

## Editing the event or its shifts

- **Event details** (title, description, location, reminder timing,
  retention window) live under **Settings** on the event page.
- **Shifts** are edited individually — expand a shift's **Edit** section to
  change its task, capacity, time window, location, or notes.
- **Deleting a shift** removes it and all of its signups; deleting the whole
  **event** removes every shift and signup in it. Both require confirmation
  and can't be undone.

## Publishing and unpublishing

Use **Publish** / **Revert to draft** at the top of the event page to
control whether volunteers can see and sign up for it. A draft event still
shows a hint that it isn't public yet, so you don't forget to publish it.

## Sharing links

- **Public link** — the event's main sign-up page, shown via **Copy link**
  or **Public link ↗**.
- **Team links** — if your club has teams, each one gets its own link (under
  **Team links** on the event page) that preselects that team, though
  volunteers can still change it.

## Statistics

The event's **Statistics** page (linked from the event header) shows:

- **Unfilled shifts** — everything still short of its capacity.
- **Shifts per team** — a breakdown by team, if your club uses them.
- **Volunteer leaderboard** — who's signed up for the most shifts, counted
  per email address. Signups whose personal data has already been
  anonymized (past the event's retention window) are counted but not
  listed by name — the leaderboard only ever shows data that still exists.

Your club also has a club-wide **Stats** page (top nav) listing every
upcoming shift with open slots across all events, most urgent first.
